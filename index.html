Birdwatching for Beginners

A Basic Guide

Why Birdwatch?

There are many reasons to birdwatch. Here are a few:

  • Birding is a low-cost hobby.
  • You can go birding virtually anywhere, at any time.
  • Birding allows you to connect with nature.
  • Birding can help you reach your step goal.
  • You can be a part of conservation efforts!

What materials do I need?

Birding doesn't require any materials. However, to get the most out of your experience, here are some things you can get:

  • Bird guide
  • Binoculars
  • A birding journal
  • The Merlin Bird ID app

Tips for Birding

If you're not sure where to start, here are 5 tips to improve your birding experience.

  1. Find a good spot
    • You can walk or stay still while birding. If you want to identify good areas for birding, many national parks have webpages that describe the birding opportunities in the area. One suggestion is to find a place where two habitats meet.
  2. Know the ideal time of day and year
    • Many birds are active throughout the day, so you aren't limited to any time of day. However, most birds sing more at dusk, so it may be easier to identify birds early in the morning.
    • Although some birds remain in the same place year-round, many birds migrate on different schedules and at differenet times of the year depending on food availability. If you are interested in viewing particular birds, research their migratory habits and common locations.
  3. Blend in
    • Camouflage is not required, however, many birders avoid wearing white. It is good practice to stay still while observing a bird and to avoid scaring them.
  4. Recognize your regulars
    • If you go to the same places at the same times, you'll likely encounter the same species repeatedly. It can be rewarding to learn their calls and features for easy recognition.
  5. Keep a journal
    • Journaling about your birding adventures can help you keep track of your observations and recognize patterns. You may be able to identify common bird movements and simply reflect on your experience.

You are ready to bird!

Don't be intimidated! Birding is simple in practice and is fun and rewarding! As you become more proficient, there are many resources to help you. Get out there and have fun!
